Call for Papers and Themes

(DE)COLONIALITY AND DIVERSITY IN HISTORIES OF EDUCATION

45 ISCHE presents as a thematic proposal the problematization of the persistence of oppressive and subalternizing educational processes, over a long historical period and at a global level, associated with resistance movements and struggles for other educations. It invites historians of education to discuss the multiple histories of education constituted in the processes of historical elaboration of social, cultural and educational geopolitics. It encourages researchers to investigate the (de)coloniality and diversity of the histories of education, through studies that discuss the organizing relational dynamics of societies that authorize oppressive universalism. Despite technological advances and scientific news, in recent decades we have experienced the advancement of social inequalities, school inequalities, precarious work, the resurgence of religious intolerance, racism and violent practices against women, indigenous peoples and LGBTQIAPN+ groups , associated with the voracity of markets, which everywhere continue, as in previous centuries, destroying nature, stealing lives and interrupting stories. By proposing the discussion of (de)coloniality and diversity in educational histories, the organizers of 45 ISCHE invite researchers to question the multiple processes of production and transmission of various knowledge, arising from experiences constituted in different geographies. It suggests discussions that can encourage questioning about the recipients of educational policies and reforms, about the training of teachers, as well as the social agencies that enabled the participation of subordinate and marginalized groups in the educational debate, in the face of the imposition of an education/ single reason.

We invite historians of education to question the epistemological, ontological and cultural diversity that organizes societies and to reflect on the dynamics of the historical constitution of school cultures: times and spaces, teaching methods, uniforms, books and school materials . In this debate, we suggest deconstructing the idea of cultural diversity based on globalized localisms, produced by hegemonic nations, and demonstrating, through historical research, the problem of the universalization of a single school, given the multiple conceptions of being in the world. In other words, decolonize the center and periphery relations, produced historically, discuss the geopolitics of education and ask about the educational and schooling dynamics at the origin of epistemological and cultural domination. Themes

To better organize the theme proposed for ISCHE 45, we suggest the following 6 axes.

  1. Colonizing processes in the history of education: agents, policies and reforms
  2. Decolonization, diversity and intersectionality in the history of education
  3. School cultures, (de)coloniality and diversity in the history of education
  4. Historiography of education: problematizing center-periphery relations
  5. Decolonizing the history of education: methods, collections and sources
  6. Diversity and (de) coloniality in teaching the history of education
